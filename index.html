Basic Exercise Principles

Introduction

For those interested in exercise science and how certain exercises affect you, the Prilepin's Chart will be essential for power and olympic lifting purposes. Those aiming for an increased personal record for lifts involving the typical bench, squat, deadlift, cling and press etc. will have to abide by a reasonable program. Details will be explained in the next paragraph.

Details Explained

Different rep ranges involve different purposes when building a program based off of the Prilepin's Chart. Muscle builidng (8-15 reps), strength (5-7), and power (1-4) all play important roles while using the Prilepin's Chart. All need to be present in a program for an appropiate duration to allow the human body to recuperate properly.

Prilepin's Chart

Percent of 1 Rep Max Reps Per Set Range of Reps
55%-65% 3-6 18-30
70%-80% 3-6 12-24
80%-90% 2-4 10-20
Above 90% 1-2 4-10

Factors To Determine Reps/Sets

  • Nutrition
  • Sleep
  • Level of Fatigue
